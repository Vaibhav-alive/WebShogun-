Clerey

Celery is a vegetable that's been cultivated for centuries and is part of the Apiaceae family, which also includes carrots, parsnip, fennel, parsley, and cumin. It has a long, fibrous stalk that tapers into leaves, and can be white to green in color, with darker colors having a stronger flavor. Celery has a mildly bitter taste and a crisp, succulent texture. You can eat celery raw or cooked, and use its stalks, leaves, or hypocotyl in cooking. Celery seeds are also used as a spice.
